7 storage tricks that keep your food fresh for longer

Simple habits that stop good food ending up in the bin

7 storage tricks that keep your food fresh for longer

By Sandar 6 June 2024

A surprising amount of food is wasted simply because it spoils before we get to it. A handful of storage habits can dramatically extend shelf life — and none of them cost a thing.

Most food does not go bad. We just forget about it.

Keep herbs upright in a glass of water like flowers. Store potatoes and onions apart, somewhere cool and dark. Freeze bread you won't finish in a few days. Keep tomatoes out of the fridge for flavour, leafy greens in it with a dry paper towel to soak up moisture, and apples away from other produce so they don't speed up ripening.

Finally, label leftovers with a date so nothing becomes a mystery at the back of the shelf. Pair these habits with a quick check of what needs eating first, and very little will ever reach the bin.
