Why a third of the world's food never gets eaten

The staggering scale of global food waste — and where it happens

Why a third of the world's food never gets eaten

By Save Food Team 12 June 2024

Every year the world produces more than enough food to feed everyone alive — and then throws away roughly a third of it. That is about 1.3 billion tonnes of edible food lost or wasted annually, from farms and factories all the way to the back of our own fridges.

We are not short of food. We are short of the will to stop wasting it.

The waste is spread across the whole journey: crops left unharvested for looking imperfect, produce bruised in transport, supermarket shelves cleared of "expired" stock, and meals scraped off plates into the bin. In wealthier countries, the single biggest culprit is the home kitchen.

The good news is that the part of the chain we control most directly — our own shopping, cooking and storage — is also the easiest to fix. Small habits add up fast, and tools like EatMeFirst make it simple to use what you already have before it spoils.
