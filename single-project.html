1.3 billion tonnes: the staggering scale of global food waste

Source

UN FAO

Scale

~1/3 of all food produced

Topic

The problem

Each year the world wastes roughly 1.3 billion tonnes of food — about one third of everything we produce. It is lost at every stage: crops left unharvested, produce rejected for looking imperfect, food spoiled in transport and storage, and meals scraped off plates into bins.

The resources behind that food are wasted too — the water, land, fuel and human labour it took to grow, harvest and ship it. Rotting in landfill, this food also releases methane, a greenhouse gas far more potent than carbon dioxide. Cutting waste is one of the most powerful and immediate things we can do for both people and the planet, and it starts with the choices we make in our own kitchens.
