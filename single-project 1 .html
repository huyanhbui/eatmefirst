Hunger in Africa: the human cost of a broken food system

Source

UN World Food Programme

Affected

~282 million people in Africa

Topic

The problem

Across Africa, an estimated 282 million people do not have enough to eat. In the worst-affected regions, families survive on a single small meal a day, and children grow up never knowing what it feels like to be truly full. Hunger stunts growth, weakens immune systems and steals a child's ability to learn and thrive.

What makes this so painful is that the world already grows more than enough food for everyone — we simply lose and waste a third of it. The food binned in one part of the world is not the same food a hungry child needs, but the resources behind it are shared: water, farmland, fuel and money. When we waste less and value food more, we help build a fairer food system where less is thrown away and more reaches the people who need it most.
