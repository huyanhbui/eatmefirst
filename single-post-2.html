The average Vietnamese family wastes 2.5 kg of food a week

A quiet habit that adds up to a national problem

The average Vietnamese family wastes 2.5 kg of food a week

By Minh Tran 10 June 2024

Vietnam is among the highest food-wasting countries in Asia. An average household throws out around 2.5 kg of food every week — rice, vegetables, meat and leftovers — often without ever noticing.

The cheapest food you will ever buy is the food you actually eat.

Multiply that across millions of homes and it becomes a national problem: wasted money for families and mountains of food rotting in landfill, releasing methane as it goes. Roughly a million đồng of food can end up in the bin each month from a single family.

Because this waste happens right in our kitchens, it is also the easiest to cut. Planning meals, storing food properly and keeping track of what you have can halve a household's waste in a matter of weeks.
