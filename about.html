About Us

On a mission to cut food waste and put wasted meals back on empty plates

What is food waste?

Food waste is any safe, edible food that ends up in the bin instead of in someone's stomach: wilted vegetables, forgotten leftovers, the loaf that went stale, the yogurt that "expired" yesterday. It happens in shops, restaurants and, most of all, in our own kitchens.

Why we built this site

Roughly one third of all food produced in the world is never eaten, about 1.3 billion tonnes every year. At the same time, hundreds of millions of people go to bed hungry. That gap isn't just sad, it's solvable. We created Save Food to explain the problem in plain language, share practical tips, and give you a free tool called EatMeFirst to waste less at home.

"Wasting food isn't just throwing away a meal. It's throwing away the water, land, fuel and labour it took to grow it."

Most household food waste is invisible: a little here, a little there, every single week. Once you start tracking it, it becomes surprisingly easy to cut down.

A child in Africa, an empty plate

While supermarkets in wealthier countries discard tonnes of "imperfect" produce, a child in parts of sub-Saharan Africa may go an entire day on a handful of food. The cruel irony of the modern food system is that we grow more than enough to feed everyone, yet we simply waste, mismanage and unevenly share it. Cutting waste won't instantly fill every plate, but it frees up food, money and resources that can.

What 2.5 kg a week really means

Vietnam is among the highest food-wasting countries in Asia. An average family quietly throws away a few kilograms of rice, vegetables, meat and leftovers every week. Multiply that across millions of households and it becomes a national problem: wasted money for families, and mountains of food rotting in landfills, releasing methane. The good news: this is the easiest kind of waste to fix, because it happens right in our own fridges.

Our solution: EatMeFirst

EatMeFirst is a simple, free tool that tackles the #1 cause of home food waste: forgetting what you already have. Add what's in your kitchen, and it sorts everything by what to eat first, warns you before food expires, suggests "use-it-up" meals, and counts the money you save from the bin. Less waste, more meals, no guilt.
