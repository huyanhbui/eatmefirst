2.5 kg a week: how much food a Vietnamese family really wastes

Focus

Vietnam

Per family

~2.5 kg wasted / week

Topic

The problem

Vietnam is one of the highest food-wasting countries in Asia. Studies estimate that an average household throws away around 2.5 kg of food every week: leftover rice, wilted vegetables, spoiled meat and dishes that were simply forgotten. Over a month, that can add up to roughly a million đồng of food going straight into the bin.

It rarely feels like waste in the moment, just a little here and a little there. But multiplied across millions of families, it becomes a national problem: money lost from household budgets, and growing mountains of food rotting in landfills. The encouraging part is that household waste is the easiest kind to fix. Buying a little less, storing food properly, and keeping track of what you already have can cut a family's waste dramatically.
