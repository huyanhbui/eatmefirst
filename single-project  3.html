Smart storage: keep your food fresh for longer

Type

Practical guide

Difficulty

Easy to start today

Topic

Take action

A lot of food is wasted simply because it spoils before we get to it, and good storage is the cheapest fix there is. A few simple habits: keep herbs like spring onions and coriander standing in a glass of water; store potatoes, onions and garlic in a cool, dark place (never next to each other); keep bananas away from other fruit because they speed up ripening; and freeze bread, meat and leftovers you won't finish in time.

Get to know your fridge, too. The door is the warmest part, so it's best for condiments, not milk. Use airtight containers to stop vegetables wilting, and keep raw meat on the bottom shelf. Most importantly, follow the "first in, first out" rule: move older items to the front so you reach for them first. That single habit is the heart of our free tool, EatMeFirst.
